About

Why did I start Homobiles? It was the 90â€™s and I ran a bike messenger service called Lickety Split All Girl Courier. The 90s turned into the 2000s and I was into music and on tour as a solo musician focused on gender. While touring the US and Europe- my mom fell ill- so I left that music scene and returned to San Francisco. My mom's car was sitting in the driveway all shiny and new, so I decided to print a few flyers, jump in my car, and drive to the nearest gay bar. I knew the clientele and I knew their needs. But what I didn't know, and what I soon learned, is that our LGBTQIA community has been ignored, bothered, assaulted, and sorely mistreated while trying to travel around San Francisco. Soon, my friends became interested and offered to start driving and suddenly our team grew into what is now Homobiles. In 2014, Homobiles received its 501 (c) 3 status and was funded solely by donations from our 5,000 passengers.

-Lynn Breedlove, founder of Homobiles
